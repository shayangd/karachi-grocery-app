## Overview

### Venture Context
**Problem**: Karachi citizens (10-12 million grocery shoppers) struggle with inconsistent grocery availability, rising prices, traffic congestion, and unpredictable store timings. The problem is especially acute for working professionals, university students, housewives, and small families who shop 3-5 times per week, making grocery shopping exhausting and time-consuming during peak hours or bad weather. Delivery delays due to traffic bottlenecks, road blocks, and rain-related issues compound the stress.

**Solution**: A hyperlocal, real-time grocery availability app partnering with neighborhood kiryana stores, minimarts, and fruit/vegetable vendors to show live inventory of essential items currently in stock. Features include dynamic delivery time estimates based on real-time traffic conditions and traffic-aware routing that avoids jammed areas and rain-affected roads. Target delivery: 30-45 minutes.

**Key Differentiators**: Real-time neighborhood store inventory + traffic-aware delivery routing (competitors don't offer this combination)

- **Secondary Goals**:

  - Identify strongest persona(s) for initial market entry

## Target Personas

- **Segment**: Individual Consumer / Household Decision Maker
- **Role**: Mid-level professional (manager, engineer, teacher, banker), ages 28-40, married or living with family
- **Household Profile**:
  - Dual-income household earning PKR 150,000-400,000/month combined

  - Lives in middle to upper-middle-class areas (Clifton, DHA, Gulshan, PECHS, Nazimabad)
  - Both partners work full-time with 9+ hour workdays
- **Why Interview**: Primary buyer persona - highest pain, best ability to pay, most time-constrained
- **Interview Focus**:
  - Emphasize Q1-Q8: Pain from traffic, time constraints, inconsistent availability during evening rush

## Section 1: Problem Discovery (Q1-Q8, ~20 minutes)
**Goal**: Validate pain intensity, recency, and workaround behavior without mentioning your solution

---

### Q1: Can you walk me through the last time you went grocery shopping or ordered groceries? What happened?
**Purpose**: Establishes recency (I1.2) and gets specific, real examples (not hypothetical)

**Probing Questions**:
- When was this exactly? (note the date/days ago)
- What did you need to buy?
- What time of day was it?
- How did it go?

**Listen for**:
- Timeframe (â‰¤7 days is ideal)
- Specific pain points emerging naturally
- Emotional cues (frustration, exhaustion, stress)

**Red Flags**:
- "I don't really shop that often" (low frequency = low priority)
- Vague answers without specifics (may be being polite)

---

### Q2: What was frustrating or difficult about that experience?
**Purpose**: Pain intensity discovery (I1.1) - let them name the pain in their own words

**Probing Questions**:
- What took longer than you expected?
- Did anything go wrong?
- How did that make you feel?
- Did this surprise you, or is this typical?

**Listen for**:
- Multiple pain points (compounding frustration)
- Intensity of language ("exhausting," "nightmare," "waste of time")
- Resignation ("it's always like this")

**Red Flags**:
- "It was fine" or minimal complaint (pain may not be severe)
- Blaming self rather than system ("I should have planned better")

---

### Q3: How often does this kind of frustration happen when you're grocery shopping?
**Purpose**: Validates problem frequency and consistency (I1.2)

**Probing Questions**:
- Is this a one-off or does this happen regularly?
- What percentage of your grocery trips have issues?
- Has it gotten worse or better over time?

**Listen for**:
- "Every time," "almost always," "at least weekly"
- Pattern recognition (e.g., "especially during monsoon," "every evening rush")
- Trend over time (getting worse = stronger pain)

**Red Flags**:
- "Rarely" or "only occasionally" (not consistent enough)
- "Used to be bad but it's better now" (problem may be resolving)

---

### Q4: On a scale of 0-10, where 0 is "no problem at all" and 10 is "extremely painful/urgent problem," how would you rate the severity of your grocery shopping challenges?
**Purpose**: Direct measurement of pain intensity (I1.1 - need â‰¥7/10)

**Probing Questions**:
- Why did you choose [number]?
- What would make it a 10?
- What's the worst part that drives that number up?
- Has it always been this bad, or has something changed?

**Listen for**:
- Score â‰¥7 with immediate, confident answer
- Specific examples justifying the score
- Multiple factors contributing to high score

**Red Flags**:
- Score <7 (insufficient pain)
- Long pause before answering (may be inflating to be nice)
- "It's not that bad, really" (contradicts score)

---

### Q5: When you face these grocery shopping problems, what do you currently do to deal with them?
**Purpose**: Identify active workarounds (I1.3 - need â‰¥50% with workarounds)

**Probing Questions**:
- Walk me through your current process step by step
- What tools, apps, or services do you use?
- Do you call ahead? Order online? Ask someone else to go?
- How much time/money does this workaround cost you?

**Listen for**:
- Active, ongoing workarounds (not just "I deal with it")
- Multiple workaround strategies tried
- Costs associated with workarounds (time, money, mental energy)
- Frustration with current workarounds

**Red Flags**:
- "I just accept it" (no active workaround = lower pain or learned helplessness)
- "I don't do anything" (may indicate low priority problem)

---

### Q6: Have you tried any delivery services or apps for groceries? Tell me about that experience.
**Purpose**: Understand competitive landscape and displacement potential (I1.5)

**Probing Questions**:
- Which ones have you tried? (Daraz, Pandamart, InstaShop, Airlift, local services)
- What did you like or dislike?
- Do you still use them? Why or why not?
- What made you stop (if applicable)?
- What's missing from these services?

**Listen for**:
- Active current usage (need to displace) vs. past trial (easier to win back)
- Specific pain points with existing solutions
- Unmet needs or gaps
- Switching barriers or reasons they stopped

**Red Flags**:
- "I love [competitor] and it's perfect" (hard to displace)
- "I tried once years ago" (not a real competitor user)

---

### Q7: Tell me about a time when you couldn't get groceries when you needed them. What happened?
**Purpose**: Captures emotional peak pain moments and consequences (I1.1, I1.2)

**Probing Questions**:
- When was this?
- What did you need?
- Why couldn't you get it?
- What did you have to do instead?
- How did this impact your day/family?

**Listen for**:
- Recent, specific story (not hypothetical)
- Real consequences (missed meal, wasted time, family frustration)
- Emotional impact (stress, guilt, anger)
- Unpredictability causing chaos

**Red Flags**:
- "Can't really remember" (not memorable = not painful enough)
- Hypothetical answer ("If this happened, I would...") instead of real story

---

### Q8: What would have to change about grocery shopping for it to be dramatically better for you?
**Purpose**: Understand desired outcomes before introducing solution (I1.6 baseline)

**Probing Questions**:
- If you had a magic wand, what's the one thing you'd fix?
- What does "better" look like specifically?
- How much time would you want to save?
- What would you do with that saved time?

**Listen for**:
- Outcomes that align with your solution (reliability, speed, inventory visibility, traffic avoidance)
- Willingness to articulate clear desired state
- Multiple outcome needs (compound value)

**Red Flags**:
- "Nothing really, it's fine" (contradicts earlier pain)
- Desired outcomes your solution can't deliver (e.g., "free groceries")

### Q9: What's your first reaction to this concept? (Open-ended)
**Purpose**: Captures unfiltered resonance without leading (I1.4 - need strong interest)

**Probing Questions**:
- What stands out to you most?
- What sounds appealing?

- How does this compare to what you do today?

**Listen for**:
- Spontaneous enthusiasm ("This would be amazing!")

- Silence or lukewarm response (red flag)

**Red Flags**:
- "That's nice" (polite but not enthusiastic)
- No follow-up questions or curiosity
- Immediate objections that kill the concept ("I would never use an app for this")

### Q10: Would this solve the problems you described earlier? Why or why not?
**Purpose**: Direct outcome alignment test (I1.6 - need â‰¥60% confirmation)

**Probing Questions**:
- Which of your frustrations would this address?
- Which problems would still exist?
- Is there anything missing that you'd need?

**Listen for**:
- Clear connection between their earlier pain and solution benefits

- Honesty about gaps (shows they're thinking critically)

**Red Flags**:

- "I'd keep doing what I do now" (no displacement)

**Probing Questions**:

**Listen for**:

### Q13: What concerns or doubts do you have about this working in Karachi?
**Purpose**: Surfaces objections, risks, and barriers to adoption

**Probing Questions**:
- What would make you skeptical about trying this?
- What could go wrong?
- Why might this not work?

**Listen for**:

- Solvable vs. unsolvable objections

**Red Flags**:
- Fundamental objections to the model ("Vendors would never agree")
- Trust issues that are very hard to overcome
- Many small objections adding up (death by a thousand cuts)

**Red Flags**:
- "Maybe in a few months" (low urgency)
- "I'd wait to see reviews" (late adopter, not early market)
- "I'd have to think about it" (low intent)
