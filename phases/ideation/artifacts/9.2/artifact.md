# Recipe 9.2: Company Intelligence Mapping

## Executive Summary

**Total Companies Mapped**: 14 across 4 categories
**Decision**: GO
**Profile Completeness**: 89% (8 standard fields)

**Key Findings**:
- **Crowded Direct Competition**: 4 well-funded direct competitors (Krave Mart $16M, Foodpanda Pandamart $10B+ parent, DealCart $7.5M, GrocerApp) with significant market share concentration
- **Critical Failure Lessons**: Airlift's $85M failure (2022) provides invaluable lessons on capital efficiency, unit economics, and the dangers of hyper-growth without profitability
- **Strategic Opportunity**: Adjacent players (InDrive, Yango, Bykea) entering grocery via partnerships rather than building organically validates asset-light approach and creates potential strategic investor/exit opportunities
- **Market Consolidation**: Post-Airlift consolidation (2022-2024) has reduced competition from 6+ players to 4 direct competitors, creating entry window for differentiated players

**Critical Intelligence Insights**:
1. **Capital Efficiency is King**: All survivors (Krave Mart, Pandamart) either have parent backing or achieved near-profitability; Airlift's failure demonstrates that $85M cannot save poor unit economics
2. **Super-App Convergence**: InDrive's $10M investment in Krave Mart (2024) and Yango's DealCart partnership (2025) show adjacent ride-hailing players view grocery as strategic super-app component
3. **Dark Store Dominance**: Market leaders (Pandamart, Krave Mart) use dark store model for 10-30 min delivery, but high capex ($2-5M per 10 stores) creates vulnerability for capital-efficient kiryana partnerships
4. **No Traffic Intelligence**: ZERO competitors emphasize traffic-aware routing despite Karachi's notorious congestion—our core differentiator is unaddressed by entire market
5. **Exit Path Validated**: Strategic acquisitions (Careem by Uber $3.1B, potential Delivery Hero interest) and corporate VC activity (InDrive, Yango) confirm acquisition exit opportunities exist
