## 8. Market Context & Competitive Dynamics

### Pakistan Quick-Commerce Funding Trends (2020-2025)

**Boom Period (2020-2022)**:
- **Total funding**: $109M in quick-commerce category (Pakistan, per Tracxn/Data Darbar)
- **Largest rounds**: Airlift $85M Series B (Aug 2021), Bazaar $70M Series B (Mar 2022)
- **Investor thesis**: COVID-19 accelerated online grocery adoption; Pakistan's 220M population underserved

**Bust Period (2022-2024)**:
- **Airlift shutdown** (July 2022): Despite $110M raised, cited global recession, VC downturn, unit economics failure
- **Funding collapse**: Pakistan e-commerce funding dropped 67% in 2023 vs 2022 (Data Darbar)
- **2024 continuation**: Further 67% drop in 2024 to $8.5M vs $26.1M in 2023

**Recovery Signals (2024-2025)**:
- **Strategic investments**: InDrive $10M → Krave Mart, Yango $10M → Trukkr
- **Super-app thesis**: Yango + DealCart partnership (grocery added to ride-hailing), InDriver exploring grocery via Krave Mart
- **Survivor focus**: Krave Mart (YC S2022) maintaining operations with "slow cash burn" and nearing profitability per press releases

### Competitive Funding Landscape

**Well-Funded Survivors**:
1. **Bazaar Technologies**: $100M+ total funding, Series B $70M (2022) — B2B focus (kiryana stores digitization)
2. **Krave Mart**: $22M total funding ($6M pre-seed, $6M seed, $10M strategic) — B2C quick-commerce, 10-15 min delivery
3. **Foodpanda/PandaMart**: Delivery Hero-backed (€318M acquisition 2016), sustained by parent company funding

**Underfunded / Bootstrapped Competitors**:
1. **DealCart**: Undisclosed funding, Yango partnership provides logistics infrastructure
2. **GrocerApp**: $6.7M total funding (per Tracxn), primarily Lahore-focused
3. **Daraz Mart**: Alibaba-owned (acquired 2018), no separate funding rounds disclosed

**Shut Down**:
1. **Airlift**: $110M total funding, shut down July 2022 after 3 years — cautionary tale for quick-commerce unit economics in Pakistan

- **Funding ask calibration**: Pre-seed $500K-$1M, Seed $3-5M, Series A $10-15M aligned with Pakistan market benchmarks

3. **Cash burn discipline**: "Slow cash burn" messaging critical (Krave Mart's success narrative)
4. **Market validation**: Early traction (orders, retention) required before institutional VC rounds
