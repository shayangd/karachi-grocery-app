6. **Interview #9** (Saddar): B2B buyer losing "a day's business revenue" when vendors fail. High ROI on reliable delivery at Rs. 200

8. **Interview #12** (Korangi): Underserved area, limited alternatives. "Delivery apps don't serve Korangi properly" - would pay for service that works

**Neutral/Need to Think (2 interviewees = 17%):**

9. **Interview #3** (PECHS): Student budget. "Willing to pay Rs. 50-100 delivery premium" suggests Rs. 149 is upper limit but acceptable. Counted as 50% acceptance
10. **Interview #5** (North Nazimabad): Lower pain (6/10), adapted schedule. "Would need to see value" suggests conditional acceptance. Counted as 50% acceptance

**Too Expensive/Not Acceptable (2 interviewees = 17%):**

11. **Interview #8** (SMCHS): Has established domestic help system costing less. May not see ROI on Rs. 149-199 delivery fee
12. **Interview #11** (Nazimabad): Values kiryana relationship over speed/convenience. Price-sensitive segment preferring traditional shopping


|------------|--------------|-------------------------|--------------|-------|
| **Krave Mart** | Rs. 50-99 (inferred) | Rs. 499+ | Not offered | "Free delivery on order above Rs. 499" promotion |

**Our Proposed Pricing:**
- Delivery Fee: Rs. 149 per order (middle of market)

### Analysis

Price acceptance at 67% exceeds the 60% threshold, indicating the market is willing to pay for the value proposition. Key insights:

1. **Value-Based Acceptance:** Interviewees accepting the price cite time savings (Rs. 500-1,000 value), reliability, and convenience - not price itself
2. **Premium Tier Validated:** Interview #6's explicit Rs. 200 willingness validates our Rs. 299 express tier for urgent needs
3. **Competitive Positioning:** Rs. 149 sits middle-of-market between budget players (DealCart free-Rs. 99) and premium (Careem Rs. 100-250)
4. **Subscription Opportunity:** 75% of interviewees shop 3-5x/week. Rs. 999/month unlimited = Rs. 250/order for 4 orders = 40% savings vs. per-order fee

**Price Sensitivity Segments:**
- **Price-Insensitive (50%):** Willing to pay Rs. 200+ for reliability (Interviews #1, #4, #6, #7, #9, #10)
- **Price-Conscious (33%):** Acceptable at Rs. 99-149 range (Interviews #2, #3, #12)
- **Price-Resistant (17%):** Prefer low-cost alternatives (Interviews #8, #11)

**Recommendation:** Lead with Rs. 149 standard delivery fee with clear value messaging ("Save 2 hours per week"). Introduce Rs. 999/month subscription at launch for 3-5x/week users. Test Rs. 299 express tier after demonstrating reliability.

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Price Sensitivity in Economic Downturn

**Risk:** Pakistan inflation (23.41% in 2024) may reduce willingness to pay for premium delivery

**Mitigation:**
- Interview data from Nov-Dec 2025 already captures post-inflation sentiment
- 100% current spend evidence shows budget exists despite inflation
- Value-based positioning (time savings) insulates from pure price competition
- Subscription model (Rs. 999) provides cost certainty for frequent users

**Monitoring:** Track conversion rates by spend tier; adjust free delivery threshold if needed

### Risk 2: Competitor Price Undercutting

**Risk:** DealCart (free delivery), Krave Mart (Rs. 499+ free) may train customers to expect Rs. 0-99 fees

**Mitigation:**
- Differentiate on value (traffic-aware routing, real-time inventory) not price
- Target mid-market (67% accept Rs. 149), not budget segment
- Interview #6: "Would pay Rs. 200 for certainty" validates premium over discounters
- Subscription (Rs. 999) provides discount for frequent users without race-to-bottom

**Monitoring:** If conversion drops below 50%, test Rs. 99-129 range

### Risk 3: Subscription Adoption Lower Than Expected

**Risk:** 30% adoption assumption may be optimistic in COD-dominant market

**Mitigation:**
- 75% of interviewees shop 3-5x/week = natural subscription candidates
- Pandapro (Rs. 999-1,299) proves market accepts subscription model
- ROI clear: 7 orders/month = break-even, 16 orders = 58% savings
- Start with per-order model, introduce subscription once repeat usage proven

**Monitoring:** Track order frequency; offer subscription after 5+ orders (revealed preference)

### Risk 4: "Show Me" Skepticism from Competitor Failures

**Risk:** 25% moderate intent (5-6/10) due to trust deficit from Pandamart/Daraz failures

**Mitigation:**
- Launch with 4-6 design partners first (Interviews #2, #6, #10 + 3 others)

- Guarantee: Full refund + Rs. 200 credit if order canceled due to stock issues

**Monitoring:** Net Promoter Score (NPS) target: 50+ (excellent for new service)

### Risk 5: Express Tier Cannibalization

**Risk:** Rs. 299 express tier may be too cheap, cannibalizing Rs. 149 standard

**Mitigation:**
- Launch express tier 3-6 months after standard (Phase 3)
- Limit express to <20% of capacity initially
- Clear messaging: Express for "urgent emergencies only"
- May need to increase to Rs. 349-399 based on demand

**Monitoring:** If express >30% of orders, raise price or limit availability
