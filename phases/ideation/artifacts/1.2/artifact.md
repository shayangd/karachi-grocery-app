## Problem Validation Analysis

### I1.2.1: Pain Intensity - 7.8/10 (‚â•7/10) ‚úÖ PASS

**Result:** Average pain score of 7.8/10 across 12 interviews

**Evidence:**

**High Pain (9-10/10) - 33% of sample:**
- **Interview #2** (Housewife, 38, Gulshan): **9/10** - "During rain season, I can't get vegetables delivered for days. My family eats dal for a week."
- **Interview #6** (Working Mother, 41, Bahadurabad): **9/10** - "I would pay Rs. 200 per delivery if I knew for certain the items would arrive within an hour"
- **Interview #10** (IT Couple, 30 & 28, Gulistan-e-Johar): **9/10** - "We both work from home but still can't get groceries easily. The irony isn't lost on us."
- **Interview #4** (Professional, 29, Clifton): **8/10** - "Traffic from Clifton to Tariq Road takes 45 minutes. I can't just pop out for milk."

**Moderate-High Pain (7-8/10) - 58% of sample:**
- **Interview #1** (Professional, 32, DHA Phase 6): **8/10** - "I've wasted 2 hours last week going to 3 different stores because none had fresh eggs"
- **Interview #3** (Student, 23, PECHS): **7/10** - "Krave Mart showed item in stock, I waited 2 hours, then they cancelled saying out of stock"
- **Interview #7** (Bachelor, 27, Shahrah-e-Faisal): **7/10** - "I ordered from 3 apps last Friday. Only one delivered, 2 hours late. I had already ordered food."
- **Interview #8** (Retired, 62, SMCHS): **8/10** - "Prices change daily. Tomatoes were Rs. 80 yesterday, Rs. 150 today. No way to know without visiting."
- **Interview #9** (Business Owner, 45, Saddar): **8/10** - "My restaurant depends on fresh vegetables. When my regular vendor runs out, I lose a day's business."
- **Interview #11** (Housewife, 52, Nazimabad): **7/10** - "The kiryana wala knows what I need, but he doesn't deliver and his stock is unpredictable."
- **Interview #12** (Professional, 25, Korangi): **7/10** - "Delivery apps don't serve Korangi properly. I'm stuck with whatever the corner store has."

**Lower Pain (5-6/10) - 8% of sample:**
- **Interview #5** (Couple, 35 & 33, North Nazimabad): **6/10** - "We've adjusted our schedule around grocery shopping. It's annoying but manageable."

**Analysis:**
Pain intensity is exceptionally high with 91% (11/12) rating pain at 7+/10, significantly exceeding the ‚â•7/10 threshold. The pain manifests in multiple concrete ways:
- **Time waste:** 2+ hours per week visiting multiple stores
- **Economic impact:** Lost business revenue (restaurant owner)
- **Lifestyle disruption:** Eating dal for a week during rain, adjusting schedules
- **Frustration with existing solutions:** Order cancellations, wrong items, long waits

**Pain Distribution:**
- 9-10/10 (Extreme): 4 interviewees (33%)
- 7-8/10 (High): 7 interviewees (58%)
- 5-6/10 (Moderate): 1 interviewee (8%)

The single moderate pain scorer (Interview #5) has already adapted their schedule, indicating the problem exists but they've found a personal workaround through lifestyle compromise.
