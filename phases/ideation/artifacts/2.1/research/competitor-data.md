## Shut-Down Case Study

### Airlift (SHUT DOWN)

**Company Profile**:
- **Company Stage**: SHUT DOWN (July 2022)
- **Founded**: 2019 (mass transit) → pivoted to quick commerce 2020
- **Headquarters**: Lahore, Pakistan (was)
- **Employees**: ~1,500 at peak (laid off at closure)

**Financial Data**:
- **Total Funding Raised**: $85 million (largest Pakistan startup funding ever)
- **Peak Valuation**: $275M
- **Final Outcome**: Complete shutdown July 2022; $85M+ burned
- **Revenue at Shutdown**: Not disclosed; reportedly never profitable
- **Customer Count**: Not disclosed
- **ARPU**: N/A

**Unit Economics** (FAILURE):
- **Burn Rate**: $200-300 per order loss reported
- **CAC**: Not disclosed but presumably very high
- **LTV/CAC**: Negative (unsustainable)
- **Root Cause**: "Every order losing 200-300 rupees" + global downturn + inability to raise follow-on funding

**Sources**:
- TikTok/Instagram posts on Airlift failure, Dec 2025
- Rest of World article on Pakistan Airlift shutdown, 2022
- Industry reports on Pakistan q-commerce consolidation

**Notes**: CRITICAL market learning. Despite $85M raised and $275M valuation, Airlift failed due to poor unit economics (losing $2-3/order), growth-at-all-costs mentality without profitability path, and global funding downturn 2022. Contrasts with Krave Mart's "near-profitability" approach (Bloomberg 2023). Key lesson: Pakistan q-commerce IS viable (Krave Mart, Pandamart prove it) but ONLY with disciplined unit economics from day 1, not growth-first mentality.

## SAM Estimation

### Method 1: Top-Down from TAM

**TAM (Total Addressable Market) - Pakistan E-Commerce**:
- Pakistan e-commerce market: Est. $5-7 billion (2024)
- Online grocery/food delivery: Est. $300-500M (subset of e-commerce)
- Q-commerce (10-30 minute delivery): Est. $50-100M (2024)
- CAGR projection: 40-50% (high growth, early stage)

**SAM Calculation** (for Karachi Grocery App):
```
SAM = TAM × Geographic % × Segment % × Use-Case %
```

**Filters**:
- **Geographic**: 60% (Karachi + major urban centers; ~60% of Pakistan urban GDP)
- **Segment**: 70% (SMB + Mid-market + Premium; excluding extreme budget)
- **Use-Case**: 85% (traffic-aware delivery applicable to most grocery delivery; excluding planned weekly shopping)

**SAM Estimate (Top-Down)**:
```
SAM = $75M (q-commerce TAM midpoint) × 0.60 × 0.70 × 0.85
SAM = $75M × 0.357
SAM ≈ $27M (2024)
```

**Projected SAM (2027)**:
```
SAM_2027 = $27M × (1.45)³ (45% CAGR over 3 years)
SAM_2027 ≈ $82M
```

### Method 2: Bottom-Up from Competitors

**Competitor Revenues in Addressable Segment** (Karachi + major urban Pakistan):

| Competitor | Est. Revenue (2024) | Addressable % | Addressable Revenue |
|------------|---------------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Foodpanda Pandamart | $50M (midpoint) | 100% | $50M |
| Krave Mart | $7.5M (midpoint) | 100% | $7.5M |
| DealCart | $3.5M (midpoint) | 100% | $3.5M |
| GrocerApp | $5.5M (midpoint) | 100% | $5.5M |
| Careem Now | $2M (estimate grocery only) | 100% | $2M |
| Daraz (grocery) | $5M (estimate Pakistan grocery subset) | 80% | $4M |

**Total SAM (Bottom-Up)**: $72.5M

### Cross-Check

- **Method 1 (Top-down)**: $27M
- **Method 2 (Bottom-up)**: $72.5M
- **Variance**: 169% (Method 2 is 2.7x Method 1)

**Reconciliation**:
- Method 2 likely OVER-estimates (includes all q-commerce, not just addressable)
- Method 1 likely UNDER-estimates (TAM base may be conservative; Pakistan e-commerce growing faster than official estimates)
- **Best Estimate**: $40-60M SAM (split the difference)

**SAM = $50M (2024)** → **$150M (2027)** at 45% CAGR
